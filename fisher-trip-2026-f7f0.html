Curaçao sits outside the hurricane belt and trades the usual Caribbean script for something more its own — Dutch colonial architecture in sorbet colors along a harbor waterfront, limestone terraces dropping to actual turquoise water, and a diving scene that draws people back year after year. Sandals Royal Curaçao sits on the island's south shore, quiet and unhurried, with the kind of beach where the water is genuinely that color. It suits couples who want a full resort experience but also a destination worth exploring — the capital Willemstad is walkable and genuinely interesting, not a souvenir strip.

My take: if you have five nights, take them. Curaçao rewards the extra day.
