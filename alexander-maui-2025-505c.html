A curated experience guide for

The Alexander Family

Maui's Upcountry coffee farms — four experiences, one family, a trip worth remembering.

Maui, Hawaiʻi July 15–22, 2025 Party of 6

Maui's Upcountry grows some of the most interesting coffee in the Pacific — not because the marketing says so, but because the elevation, volcanic soil, and trade winds actually produce something distinct. The four options below range from a morning brunch on a working farm to a private two-hour deep-dive into chocolate and coffee science. Read through what each offers and think about what a "great experience" looks like for your family — then let Andrea know which direction feels right.

Before you decide
What kind of experience are you after?
If you want a meal included

O'o Farm is the move — the farm-to-table brunch or lunch is the centerpiece, coffee is the lens. You're eating well while learning.

If you want pure coffee focus

Shim Farm (when available) is the most hands-on, unscripted coffee experience. Seed to roasted bean, no tourists, no agenda.

If you want chocolate too

Maui Chocolate & Coffee Tours combines both — great for families with varying interests. Rare chocolates from 10+ countries plus 100% Maui coffee.

If you want a longer morning

O'o Farm runs 3 hours with a full sit-down meal. Maui Chocolate & Coffee is 2 hours. Shim Farm is relaxed and self-paced.

Option 1
3 hours Mon–Fri · 8:30 am Brunch included Upcountry Kula
O'o Farm — Seed-to-Cup Coffee & Brunch
651 Waipoli Road, Kula · Slopes of Haleakalā

The tour starts before the farm walk — with French press coffees and house-made coffee-cherry chocolate truffles while a guide explains the history of the farm and how coffee moves from cherry to cup. Then a walk through lush coffee orchards before the main event: a family-style farm-to-table brunch from ingredients harvested that morning. The menu rotates weekly but typically includes a seasonal pastry, garden frittata with Maui Nui venison, roasted vegetables, and finishes with a pour-over coffee and native māmaki tisane. Vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free accommodated with advance notice.

Option 2
3 hours Mon–Fri · 10:30 am 4-course lunch Upcountry Kula
O'o Farm — Gourmet Farm-to-Table Lunch
651 Waipoli Road, Kula · Slopes of Haleakalā

A longer, more elaborate version of the O'o Farm experience that swaps the morning coffee focus for a four-course family-style lunch. The walk covers vegetable fields, edible flowers, and fruit orchards with a regenerative farming lens before the meal — sourdough focaccia and salad, fresh fish with roasted vegetables, seasoned chicken with seasonal rice, and dessert with coffee. The menu changes weekly based on what's in season. If your family is more interested in a farm-to-table lunch experience and less fixated on the coffee-specific education, this is the better fit.

  • Coffee served at the close of the meal

Option 3
2 hours Private group Chocolate + Coffee Kula · Kupa'a Farm
Maui Chocolate & Coffee Tours — Chocolate and Coffee Adventure
1886 Na'alae Road, Kula · Kupa'a Organic Farm

This one is entirely private — just the Alexander family, your guide, and an organic cacao and coffee orchard on the slopes of Haleakalā. The experience draws a direct parallel between cacao and coffee: how both begin as fruit, both require a lengthy transformation, and both produce something the rest of the world is obsessed with. You'll taste cacao pulp straight from the pod, move through dried beans and roasted nibs, sample 6–7 chocolates from different growing regions around the world, and sip 100% Maui-grown coffee. There's also a hands-on ceremonial cacao drink component and — bonus — farm animals (goats, sheep, a deer, and birds) roaming the property. Bi-coastal views of Maui from the hilltop.

What you'll actually do
  • Private guided walk through cacao grove and coffee orchard (just your party)
  • Cacao tasting: fresh pulp → dried beans → roasted nibs
  • Global chocolate tasting — 6–7 varieties including Hawaiian chocolate
  • Hand-craft a traditional ceremonial cacao drink
  • Coffee tasting — 100% Maui-grown, low acid with tropical fruit notes
  • Meet the farm animals · bi-coastal views of Maui

Self-paced By appointment One-man farm Kula · 3,300 ft
Shim Farm — Coffee Farm Tour with Casey Shim
Kula's Upcountry Chinatown · 3,300–3,500 ft above sea level
Note from Andrea: This is one of Maui's most personal farm experiences and the guest reviews are exceptional — multiple visitors called it the highlight of their trip. The season runs February through August, so July falls right in range. I'm confirming current availability before recommending it as bookable. If it's open, it's worth serious consideration.

Casey Shim is the third generation of his family to work this land — his grandparents were Chinese immigrants who settled in Kula's Chinatown area decades ago. He runs the entire farm solo: growing coffee, protea flowers, and dozens of botanical plants on a property perched with a million-dollar view of the island. There's no script here. Casey walks guests through the complete coffee process — picking, pulping, fermenting, rinsing, drying, and roasting — with as much time as the conversation needs. The farm also has horses in the pasture, the original family home still standing, and a section of Hawaiian plants.

What you'll actually do
  • Full coffee process: cherry → pulping → fermenting → drying → roasting
  • See the pulper, huller, drying trays, and roaster up close
  • Learn the farm's family history spanning three generations
  • Aromatherapy garden, Hawaiian plants section, fruit trees
  • Meet the horses · see the original family farmhouse
  • No rush, no schedule — deeply personal and unscripted
