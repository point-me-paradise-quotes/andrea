What to Know Before You Choose

Autism on the Seas — Both Sailings

Sailing A and Sailing B are both staff-assisted group sailings through Autism on the Seas, which works alongside Royal Caribbean's own autism-friendly program to support families with autism, Down syndrome, and other developmental disabilities. Families on either sailing may have access to additional trained staff and support services beyond the standard onboard program.

Royal Caribbean's onboard autism-friendly offerings include:

  • Expedited check-in, boarding, and departure
  • Autism awareness-trained youth staff
  • Flexible Adventure Ocean age-group placement based on ability, when appropriate
  • Special dietary accommodations
  • Autism-friendly toy lending program
  • Sensory-friendly movie showings
  • Social stories to help prepare guests before sailing

Why Families Choose Symphony of the Seas

Symphony of the Seas is one of the largest ships sailing today, with multiple pools, waterslides, the Ultimate Abyss dry slide, mini golf, laser tag, Broadway-style shows, ice skating, youth programs, and a long list of dining venues. Both sailings visit a similar run of Western Caribbean ports — Roatan, Costa Maya, and Cozumel — each offering its own mix of beach time, reef snorkeling, and shore excursions.
