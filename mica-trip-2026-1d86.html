Step Two

Choose Your Resort

All three sit within a few minutes of each other in Playa Mujeres, the gated stretch of coast north of the Cancún hotel zone — and all three share the area's calmest, clearest water. The differences are in scale, style, and who they're built for. Read through each, then pick a room.

Why Playa Mujeres over the Cancún strip — we steered this quote toward Playa Mujeres on purpose. The beach here runs noticeably cleaner, with far less of the seaweed buildup that's become common on the Cancún hotel-zone strip and Riviera Maya in recent years. The trade-off is location: Playa Mujeres sits a bit north, so the airport transfer runs about 35–40 minutes instead of the 15–20 you'd get staying right on the strip. For a short 3-night trip, we think the better beach is worth the extra 20 minutes each way — but wanted you to know exactly why we pointed you here.

A Note on Tipping

All-inclusive pricing above does not include discretionary tipping for bartenders, servers, and housekeeping. It isn't required, but it's customary and appreciated. A reasonable budget for 2 people over 3 nights is about $90–$120 in small bills — $1s, $5s, or pesos work best. Plan to bring cash with you for the resort.
